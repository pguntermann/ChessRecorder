# Privacy Policy for Chess Recorder

**Effective date:** 17 July 2026  
**Developer:** Philipp Guntermann  
**Contact:** [pguntermann@me.com](mailto:pguntermann@me.com)  
**App:** Chess Recorder (iOS / iPadOS)

This privacy policy describes how Chess Recorder handles information when you use the app.

## Summary

Chess Recorder is designed to work on your device. The developer does **not** operate backend servers that collect, sell, or share your personal data for advertising or analytics.

## Data stored on your device

The app may store the following locally on your device (for example in app storage or settings):

- Recorded chess games and related notation (including PGN)
- App settings and preferences
- Custom speech phrases / vocabulary you teach the app
- Optional move-assessment and analysis results generated on device

This data stays on your device unless **you** choose to export, copy, or share it (for example via Share Sheet or Files).

You can remove local data by deleting the app (and, if applicable, clearing shared exports you created yourself).

## Microphone and speech recognition

Chess Recorder uses the microphone and Apple’s speech recognition APIs so you can dictate chess moves.

- Audio is processed to convert speech into text for move entry.
- The app prefers **on-device** recognition when the device and language support it.
- Speech recognition is provided by **Apple**. Depending on your device, system settings, and language support, processing may follow Apple’s on-device or Apple cloud speech services under [Apple’s Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/privacy/).
- The developer of Chess Recorder does **not** receive your microphone audio or transcripts on developer-operated servers.

Microphone and speech access are optional for core board use if you enter moves by touch instead of dictation. You can revoke permissions in iOS Settings → Chess Recorder.

## Engine analysis

Optional Stockfish-based analysis and move assessment run **on your device**. Positions and evaluations are not uploaded to the developer.

## Data the developer does not collect

Chess Recorder does **not**, through developer-operated services:

- Create user accounts
- Run third-party advertising or analytics SDKs
- Track you across other companies’ apps or websites
- Sell personal information

## Crash and diagnostics data

If you have enabled sharing analytics/diagnostics with app developers in iOS Settings, Apple may provide anonymized crash or diagnostic reports to the developer through Apple. That process is controlled by Apple; Chess Recorder does not implement a separate crash-reporting SDK.

## Children

Chess Recorder does not target children for data collection. It does not knowingly collect personal information from children through developer-operated services.

## Third-party components

The app includes open-source components (for example chess rules, Stockfish/LucidEngine, opening data, and piece artwork). Those components run on device as part of the app. See [THIRD_PARTY_LICENSES.md](THIRD_PARTY_LICENSES.md) in the project repository.

Links opened from About & Help (for example GitHub or project websites) are handled by the system browser; those sites have their own privacy policies.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Effective date” at the top will be revised when material changes are made. The current version is published in the [Chess Recorder GitHub repository](https://github.com/pguntermann/ChessRecorder).
